About Hover Africa

A Community Built on Care & Purpose

Hover Africa is a youth-centered community focused on empowering teenagers and young adults across Africa to live mentally healthy, confident, and purposeful lives. We believe every young person deserves support, a safe space, and the tools to thrive.

From mental health conversations to digital empowerment programs, community events to outreach initiatives — we're building the next generation of resilient African leaders.

The Initiative

What is Flow with Hover?

Flow with Hover is Hover Africa's menstrual health and dignity initiative. We believe menstruation should never be a barrier to education, confidence, or dignity.

Through this program, we provide menstrual products, age-appropriate health education, and emotional support to school-going girls — helping them stay in class and stand tall.

Our Children's Day Pad Drive focuses specifically on reaching girls in underserved communities, ensuring they have what they need to show up fully to their lives.

🩸
Menstrual Care Support
Pads, hygiene kits, and practical support distributed directly to girls who need them.
📚
Health Education
Age-appropriate education about menstrual health, body positivity, and self-care.
💪
Confidence Building
Workshops and conversations that help girls feel empowered and proud of who they are.
Why It Matters

The Problem We're Solving

📉
Girls Miss School Every Month

1 in 10 girls in sub-Saharan Africa misses school during their period due to lack of menstrual products. Over time, this leads to falling behind — and dropping out.

😔
Confidence & Dignity at Stake

Without proper access to pads, girls feel ashamed, anxious, and less than. This affects their mental health, self-esteem, and ability to participate fully in life.

💸
Period Poverty is Real

Many families simply cannot afford menstrual products each month. Girls are forced to improvise with unsafe materials, putting their health at risk.

🏫
Schools Are Unprepared

Most schools lack proper sanitation, menstrual health education, and the resources to support girls during their period. We're here to bridge that gap.
